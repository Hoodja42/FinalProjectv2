Listen: Nebraska Swimmers Speed Up As Olympic Trials head for Indianapolis

How has the Nebraska swimming community changed since 2008?

By: Andrew Hood

5/8/23

Since 2008, the capitol of the American swimming world has been Omaha, Nebraska. Here, the top 1000 swimmers in the country gather each Olympiad in hopes of securing one of the coveted spots on the US Olympic team. Michael Phelps, Katie Ledecky, Caleb Dressel Simone Manuel and many other Olympic Champions have all cut their teeth in the Omaha water.

But that’s about to change. USA Swimming, the sport’s governing body announced last year that the trials would have a new home in 2024 – The Indianapolis Colts’ Lucas Oil Stadium – leaving the local swimming community without what has become its marquee event. While economic and social impacts of such a large event leaving town are significant, I’ve become interested in a more athletic question — did hosting the trials make Nebraska swimmers faster?
